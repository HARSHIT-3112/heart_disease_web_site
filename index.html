About Heart Disease

Understanding Cardiovascular Health

Heart disease is a group of diseases related to cardiovascular conditions, manifested by a violation of the normal functioning of the heart. It may be caused by damage to the epicardium, pericardium, myocardium, endocardium, valvular apparatus of the heart, and heart vessels.

According to the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute in Framingham (USA), the most important factors in the development of cardiovascular disease in humans are obesity, sedentary lifestyle, and smoking.

Leading Cause

Heart disease is the #1 cause of death globally, accounting for 32% of all deaths.

Risk Factors

Obesity, smoking, and physical inactivity are among the top preventable risk factors.

Early Detection

Proper screening and data analysis can significantly improve early diagnosis rates.

Prevention

Up to 80% of heart disease cases can be prevented through lifestyle changes.
